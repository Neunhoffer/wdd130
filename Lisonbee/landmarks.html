Ferris Wheel Description - One of the most recognizable landmarks featured in The Last of Us Part II is the Seattle Great Wheel. In real life, the Ferris wheel sits along the waterfront in Seattle and is a bright, lively attraction that offers views of the city skyline and Elliott Bay. In the game, however, the Ferris wheel becomes part of a much darker landscape. Surrounded by abandoned buildings, stormy skies, and an overgrown shoreline, the once-busy landmark appears silent and isolated. This contrast helps show how the game’s environmental design transforms familiar places into reminders of a world that has been changed by time and disaster.

The Space Needle Decsription - The Space Needle is one of the most famous landmarks in Seattle and has long been a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors who want to see panoramic views of the city from above. In the world of The Last of Us Part II, however, the Space Needle no longer holds that same importance. While it can still be seen in the distant skyline, it is no longer a destination or point of interest for the characters. Instead, it becomes just another forgotten structure in a city overtaken by nature and decay. This subtle change highlights how the game's environmental design shows the passage of time and the loss of the vibrant life that once filled the city.

The Seattle Aquarium Description - The Seattle Aquarium is another real-world location that appears in The Last of Us Part II. In real life, the aquarium is a popular attraction along the waterfront in Seattle, where visitors can see marine animals and learn about the ecosystems of the Pacific Northwest. In the game, however, the aquarium has been abandoned and repurposed. Instead of being filled with tourists and exhibits, it becomes a quiet refuge used by characters trying to survive in a dangerous world. This transformation shows how familiar public spaces in Seattle were redesigned by the developers to fit the harsh realities of the post-apocalyptic setting while still keeping recognizable elements of the original location.
